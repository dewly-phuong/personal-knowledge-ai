# Memex

The **Memex** is a conceptual device envisioned by Vannevar Bush in his 1945 essay "As We May Think". It describes a personal, curated knowledge store designed to extend human memory and facilitate the retrieval and association of information.

## Technical Definition

Bush's Memex was conceived as a desk-like device that would allow individuals to store all their books, records, and communications. It featured mechanisms for rapid retrieval, annotation, and, most notably, the creation of "associative trails." These trails allowed users to link related documents and ideas together, creating a custom, interconnected web of knowledge that reflected their own thought processes.

Key characteristics of the Memex included:
*   **Personal**: Intended for individual use and curation.
*   **Curated**: Content would be actively selected and organized by the user.
*   **Associative Trails**: The ability to establish dynamic links between disparate pieces of information, making the connections between documents as valuable as the documents themselves.
*   **Active Indexing**: A system for organizing and retrieving information rapidly.

## Relationship to LLM-Driven Wikis

The concept of the Memex is closely related in spirit to modern patterns for building personal knowledge bases, such as the [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki]] pattern. Bush's vision anticipated many features now sought in digital knowledge management. However, a significant challenge Bush couldn't solve was the immense burden of maintaining such a meticulously curated and interconnected knowledge base.

In the context of LLM-driven wikis, Large Language Models (LLMs) are proposed as the solution to this maintenance problem. While [[wiki/person/vannevar-bush]] envisioned a human-intensive process for creating and maintaining associative trails, LLMs can automate the summarizing, cross-referencing, updating, and contradiction flagging across a large corpus of documents, significantly reducing the human effort required to keep a knowledge base current and interconnected. This allows the wiki to be a "persistent, compounding artifact" that grows richer with every source added, fulfilling the spirit of the Memex with automated assistance.
