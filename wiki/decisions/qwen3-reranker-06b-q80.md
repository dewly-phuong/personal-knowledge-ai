# Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-Q8_0

The Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-Q8_0 is a local [[wiki/concepts/gguf]] model primarily used within [[wiki/services/qmd]] (Query Markup Documents) for the purpose of re-ranking search results. It is a critical component of QMD's hybrid search pipeline, enhancing the relevance of retrieved documents by providing a fine-grained assessment.

## Purpose and Functionality

This model functions as an LLM cross-encoder reranker. It takes a set of candidate documents, typically the top 30 generated from Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF), and evaluates their relevance to a given query. The reranker outputs a relevance score for each document, ranging from 0.0 to 1.0, and sorts them accordingly.

## Technical Details

*   **Type**: [[wiki/concepts/gguf]] model.
*   **Size**: Approximately 640MB.
*   **Integration**: It leverages [[wiki/concepts/node-llama-cpp]]'s `createRankingContext()` and `rankAndSort()` API for its operations.
*   **Deployment**: Automatically downloaded from HuggingFace on its first use by [[wiki/services/qmd]] and stored in the local cache directory (`~/.cache/qmd/models/`).
*   **Score Conversion**: Its raw LLM rating (on a scale of 0-10) is internally converted to a 0.0-1.0 range for consistency in the fusion strategy.

## Architectural Role in QMD

Within the [[wiki/services/qmd]] hybrid search pipeline, the Qwen3-Reranker-0.6B-Q8_0 plays a significant role in refining search results:

1.  **Candidate Selection**: After the initial stages of query expansion, parallel BM25 and vector searches, and subsequent RRF fusion, a selection of approximately 30 top candidate documents is passed to the reranker.
2.  **LLM Re-ranking**: The model performs an LLM-based re-ranking, providing a relevance judgment (effectively "yes/no" with associated logprob confidence) for each document.
3.  **Position-Aware Blending**: The scores generated by the reranker are then blended with the RRF scores using a position-aware strategy to determine the final document scores:
    *   For documents initially ranked 1-3 by RRF: The final score is 25% from the reranker and 75% from retrieval. This preserves strong initial matches.
    *   For documents initially ranked 4-10 by RRF: The final score is 40% from the reranker and 60% from retrieval.
    *   For documents initially ranked 11 and beyond by RRF: The final score is 60% from the reranker and 40% from retrieval. This places more trust in the reranker for less obvious matches.
