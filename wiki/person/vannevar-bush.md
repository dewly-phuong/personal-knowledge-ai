# Vannevar Bush

## Overview

Vannevar Bush (1890-1974) was an American engineer, inventor, and science administrator, best known for his conceptual device called the [[wiki/concepts/memex]], described in his 1945 essay "As We May Think". The Memex envisioned a personal, curated knowledge store where an individual could store all their books, records, and communications, complete with "associative trails" linking documents together.

## Relevance to LLM Wiki

The concept of an [[wiki/concepts/llm-wiki]] directly draws inspiration from Bush's Memex. Bush's vision was for a system that was private, actively curated, and where the connections between documents were as valuable as the documents themselves. The primary challenge Bush couldn't solve was the immense burden of maintaining such an intricate web of knowledge and its cross-references. Modern LLMs are positioned to address this maintenance problem, enabling the creation of persistent, compounding knowledge bases that automate the summarizing, cross-referencing, filing, and bookkeeping that humans typically find tedious.
